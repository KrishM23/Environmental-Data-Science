# Environmental Data Science

Terrain is a starting point for getting into environmental data science at UCLA. I built it because when I tried to get into this field as a first-year, there wasn't one place that pointed me to the right datasets, the faculty actually doing this work, or a project I could just sit down and do. So I made that place.

Everything here is free and runs on free tools. No paid software, no powerful laptop needed, and no prior coding experience assumed for the beginner stuff.

## What's on the site

- **Datasets** a curated library of real environmental datasets (CalEnviroScreen, EPA AirNow, NOAA, NASA Earthdata, USGS, and UCLA's own emissions data), each with a plain-English explainer and what kind of question it can answer.
- **Starter projects** guided projects in R or Python, sorted by level from total beginner to advanced. Each one is built on a real dataset and ends with something you actually made: a map, a chart, a model.
- **Faculty** a directory of UCLA researchers across departments who work in environmental data, so you know who to reach out to and why.
- **Carbon tracker** a live chart tracking UCLA's actual emissions against its 2025 carbon-neutrality pledge, built from the university's public reports.

## Who it's for

Mainly students who want to do real environmental work but don't know where to start, with a focus on making it accessible to first-gen, low-income, and underrepresented students who don't already have a way in.

## Built with

Plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Chart.js for the tracker. No framework, no build step. The whole thing is static, which keeps it fast, free to host, and easy for other people to contribute to.

## Status

Early but real. The site and its content are live; the project walkthrough videos and notebooks are being added. This is a UCLA DataX student initiative and it's growing, so if you want to help build it, add a dataset, or write a starter project, get in touch.
